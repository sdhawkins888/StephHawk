Getting around

Every route below starts at the front door of 4300 Avenue Laval.

🚶 Walking to Mont-Royal Métro ORANGE LINE

  1. Exit 4300 Avenue Laval and turn left (north) onto Ave Laval.
  2. Walk straight up Laval for about 4 short blocks — you'll pass rows of the Plateau's painted greystones and spiral staircases (a good slow-poke distraction for the kids).
  3. When Laval ends at Avenue du Mont-Royal, turn right (east).
  4. Walk one block to Rue Rivard. The métro entrance is right on that corner (470 Mont-Royal E, at Place Gérald-Godin).
  5. Head down into the station and tap in for the Orange Line.
~7 minute walk at adult pace — budget 12–15 with the kids. Direction "Côte-Vertu" takes you toward downtown/Old Montreal; direction "Montmorency" goes the other way.

🗺️ Mont-Royal Métro → Old Montreal ORANGE LINE

  1. At Mont-Royal station, board the Orange Line toward Côte-Vertu.
  2. Ride 5 stops (Sherbrooke, Berri-UQAM, Champ-de-Mars, Place-d'Armes, Square-Victoria–OACI) to Square-Victoria–OACI for September Café, or
  3. get off 2 stops earlier at Champ-de-Mars to walk straight into Old Montreal / the Old Port.
Champ-de-Mars puts you right at the edge of Old Montreal, a minute from City Hall and Place Jacques-Cartier.

🥞 Old Montreal walking route (pancakes → sights → Old Port)

September Café itself sits a little west of Old Montreal proper, in Griffintown — so start there, then walk the sights on your way back east. Total walking is roughly 1.5–2 km; plenty of benches and stroller-friendly cobblestone-adjacent sidewalks along the way.

  1. September Café — pancakes

    Get off at Square-Victoria–OACI, walk 5 min to 2471 Rue Notre-Dame O. Go early (opens can mean lines by mid-morning).

  2. Notre-Dame Basilica (exterior / quick peek)

    Walk ~15 min east along Notre-Dame to Place d'Armes. The façade alone is worth the stroller detour.

  3. Place Jacques-Cartier

    One block over — a sloped, pedestrian square with street performers and flower stalls; easy for kids to run around.

  4. Hôtel de Ville & Château Ramezay

    Grand City Hall building and the small stone château next door, both just photo-stops.

  5. Marché Bonsecours

    The silver-domed market building — a good spot for a bathroom break and a snack.

  6. Old Port — Voiles en Voiles & La Grande Roue

    Down at the water: the climbing-course park (has courses scaled for young kids) and the big Ferris wheel, right next to each other on Quai King-Edward / Quai Jacques-Cartier.

Champ-de-Mars métro (Orange Line) is a 5-minute walk back from Marché Bonsecours — your quickest way home when the rain rolls in.

⛰️ Up Mont Royal (chalet + Beaver Lake) BUS 11

  1. Walk to Mont-Royal métro station (same route as above).
  2. At street level, catch Bus 11 (Montagne / Parc du Mont-Royal) right outside the station.
  3. Ride up the mountain — the bus does the climbing for you, no need to tackle the stairs with short legs.
  4. Get off near the Chalet du Mont-Royal lookout, or continue toward Beaver Lake (Lac aux Castors), where there's a playground right next to the water.
This is the family-friendly way up — skips the steep climb entirely. The chalet terrace has one of the best views of downtown.

🧬 To the Biodome & Insectarium ORANGE LINE GREEN LINE

  1. Walk to Mont-Royal métro station.
  2. Board Orange Line toward Côte-Vertu, ride 2 stops to Berri-UQAM.
  3. At Berri-UQAM, change to the Green Line toward Honoré-Beaugrand.
  4. Ride 7 stops to Viau — the Biodome and Insectarium are right at the station exit.
One transfer, same fare. Good indoor rainy-morning option since both buildings are climate-controlled.

🏊 To Sir Wilfrid Laurier Park (pool day)

Turn left out the front door, walk north on Laval to Laurier Ave Est, then right (east) on Laurier to de Brébeuf — the pool entrance is at that corner. About 20–25 minutes at kid pace.

✈️ Airport transfers

Montréal–Trudeau (YUL) to 4300 Ave Laval is about a 20–25 minute taxi ride (Montreal airport taxis run a flat rate to most downtown/Plateau addresses — ask the dispatcher to confirm before you get in). Taxi stands are just outside the arrivals doors.

Mont-Royal Avenue

The Plateau's main street — pedestrian-only all summer, and a 2-minute walk from the house. These picks (via Tourisme Montréal) run west to east, so they're listed in walking order from your corner.

How to walk it: Laval meets Mont-Royal one block from the house. Turn right (east) and everything below unfolds in order — the closest shop is 2 minutes away, the farthest about 25 at kid pace. Stroller-friendly the whole way, with plenty of benches, picnic tables, and ice cream between stops.
